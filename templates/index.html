Badgers in Ireland: A Peek into Their World

Badgers (Meles meles) are iconic nocturnal mammals, deeply woven into the fabric of Irish wildlife. They are fascinating creatures, known for their distinctive black and white striped faces and powerful digging claws, which they use to construct elaborate underground homes called 'setts'.

In Ireland, badgers thrive in a variety of habitats, from woodlands and hedgerows to farmland. They are omnivores, with a diet primarily consisting of earthworms, but also include insects, fruits, roots, and small mammals. Observing badgers in their natural habitat can be a rare treat, revealing their diligent foraging and social behaviours.

Our badger watch aims to provide a glimpse into the secret lives of these shy creatures in Terrerath, helping us better understand and appreciate their presence in our local ecosystem.
